1. COUNCIL PROFILE — Legal Identity and Incorporation: The Apex Global Export Council, known as AGC, is a Section 8 Company under the Companies Act 2013 (formerly Section 25 under the 1956 Act). It is a non-profit organization established in accordance with the Foreign Trade Policy under the Foreign Trade (Development and Regulation) Act, 1992. It was notified by the Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Department of Commerce, Government of India. It represents the National Export Industry, specifically Consumer Goods, Industrial Materials, and Specialty Products.
1. COUNCIL PROFILE — Historical Growth and Member Reach: Since its incorporation in 1963, export promotion strategies have yielded high growth rates. These achievements are the result of dedicated efforts from approximately 4,000+ AGC members. The Council unites manufacturers and exporters across India for a common interest. It aims to shape and influence the industry's future while protecting customer interests.
1. COUNCIL PROFILE — Industry Positioning and Opportunities: India is recognized as a significant sourcing hub for export products. The technical expertise and entrepreneurial skills of Indian businessmen, coupled with Council support, allow the industry to capitalize on global trade opportunities. The Council's primary goal is to personify the vision of excellence in positioning Indian exports as products of choice globally.
1. COUNCIL PROFILE — Vision Statement: The vision is to excel in Positioning Basic Exports from India as products of choice in the regional and global markets.
1. COUNCIL PROFILE — Mission Statement: The mission is to enable and assist members in enhancing exports of products to realize full potential in global markets through concerted efforts and deliver duties as a nodal government agency.
1. COUNCIL PROFILE — Objectives (Interface and Alliances): A primary objective is to act as an interface between the Industry and the Government of India to formulate export-oriented policies. The Council creates a favorable environment to augment exports of items from India. It participates in international exhibitions held abroad and invites overseas delegations to India to establish long-term strategic alliances.
1. COUNCIL PROFILE — Objectives (Sponsorship and Consultancy): The Council sponsors trade delegations, study teams, and sales teams to various markets abroad. It organizes workshops and seminars focused on trade and policy issues. It provides dedicated consultancy and guidance services specifically for exporters.
1. COUNCIL PROFILE — Objectives (Member Services and REACH): The Council assists members in pinpointing sources for their requirements and helps facilitate smooth, free trade deals between buyers and sellers. It serves as a forum for representing trade-related issues. It is the nodal agency appointed by the Ministry of Commerce & Industry for compliance with international trade legislation.
1. COUNCIL PROFILE — Objectives (Liaison and Grievances): The Council participates in Grievance Committee Meetings organized by the DGFT to take up member issues regarding Customs, Central Excise, and Port Authorities. It participates in Review Meetings and Task Force Meetings with the Ministry to chalk out export promotion strategies. It prepares and submits Annual Action Plans and Budget Proposals to the Ministry for international exhibitions.
1. COUNCIL PROFILE — Objectives (Logistics and Recognition): The Council issues recommendation letters to overseas Consulates/Embassies for member Visas for business promotion tours. It issues Non-preferential Certificates of Origin for items manufactured in India. It organizes Export Award Functions to recognize outstanding export performance. It aims to be a (one contact point) for sourcing any products from India.
1. COUNCIL PROFILE — Confidentiality (Employee Obligation): By accepting employment, all employees agree not to disclose confidential or trade information to third parties. This obligation remains binding even after an employee leaves the Council. Confidentiality is governed by the general employment terms and conditions applicable to all staff.
1. COUNCIL PROFILE — Confidentiality (Definition and Scope): Confidential information includes any form: written, electronic, oral, overheard, or observed. It specifically includes internal trade and financial information, Council plans, and private data concerning members, suppliers, and employees. Access is granted strictly on a (need to know) basis.
1. COUNCIL PROFILE — Confidentiality (Daily Conduct and Storage): Confidential information must not be discussed with other employees, trade members, vendors, family, or friends. Confidential documents must be kept in folders or envelopes marked (CONFIDENTIAL) with the custodian's name and designation. Extremely sensitive files like personnel and financial records must be kept in locked cupboards or safes.
1. COUNCIL PROFILE — Confidentiality (Custody and Penalties): Keys to confidential safes are kept by the Director General or a competent authority. Disclosure of confidential information, whether willful or accidental, is a serious offense leading to strict disciplinary action. The HR manual may be uploaded on the Council intranet with restricted downloading and printing to maintain security.

3. ATTENDANCE & WORKING POLICY — Policy Framework: This policy document was proposed on 10/02/2022 and became effective on 08/01/2024. it supersedes the previous Service Rules document dated 10/06/1968. The policy was approved by the Director General, Vice-Chairman, Chairman, and CoA.
3.1 ATTENDANCE POLICY — Objective and Registration: AGC expects all employees to be ready to work at the beginning of assigned daily hours and to complete tasks by the end of those hours. All employees of all grades must register their attendance daily at the scheduled reporting time. Salary will not be paid for any day if attendance has not been registered in person. Regular attendance is considered essential for efficient workflow and serves as a measure of an employee's desire to perform their job.
3.1 ATTENDANCE POLICY — Punctuality and Reporting Standards: The Council does not tolerate repeated absenteeism or late coming, as these result in low productivity and indiscipline. It is the responsibility of every employee to report for duty at the fixed or notified time and meet attendance standards. The Director General approves the monthly attendance of staff and sends the report to Payroll on the 25th of every month.
3.1 ATTENDANCE POLICY — Regular Office Timings: The regular office timings at all locations are 09.30 a.m. to 6.00 p.m.. These hours include a half-hour lunch break. This attendance and working policy applies to all employees of AGC.
3.3 & 3.4 ATTENDANCE POLICY — Late Arrival and Early Departure Permissions: For personal exigencies, employees may be permitted to report late or leave early with HoD permission and intimation to the HR Department. If an employee works late until 01:00 a.m. or beyond, they are permitted to report to the office at 13:00 hrs (1:00 PM) the next day without half-day salary deduction. However, employees exceeding this specific time limit must apply for a half-day leave for every such instance.
3.5 ATTENDANCE POLICY — Emergency Leave Procedures: In cases of emergency situations or ill health, employees must inform their superior over the phone. Upon returning to duty, the employee must submit a leave application to their immediate reporting manager immediately. All Casual Leave (CL) and Privilege Leave (PL) must be sanctioned in advance, except for emergency casual leaves notified by phone, which are subject to post-approval at the HoD's discretion.
3.6 ATTENDANCE POLICY — Formal Approval Process: To obtain permission for late coming or early leaving, an employee must write an application and get approval from their reporting superior. The approved application is then submitted to the HR Department for acknowledgment. Regional Directors are responsible for approving these requests at regional offices. Employees should keep a duplicate copy of the application for their reference. Discrepancies must be reported to the Director General. While punctuality is required, emergency late arrivals or early departures are allowed twice a month with HoD approval.
3.7 ATTENDANCE POLICY — Late Sitting Facilities: Facilities are available for individual employees working beyond 20:00 hrs (8:00 PM). Late sitting is only permitted with prior permission from superiors and is at the sole discretion of Management based on work exigencies.
3.7 ATTENDANCE POLICY — Late Sitting Conveyance Allowance (M1–M13): Grade M1-M5 employees are eligible for Cab/Taxi fare as actuals upon submission of the bill if not using an office vehicle. Grade M6-M13 employees are eligible for Cab/Taxi fare as actuals with HOD approval, possibly on a sharing basis with other employees.
3.7 ATTENDANCE POLICY — Late Sitting Conveyance Allowance (E1–E3): Grade E1-E3 staff are eligible for actual Cab/Taxi fare up to Rs. 800 with HOD approval. This allowance may be provided on a sharing basis with other employees.
3.8 ATTENDANCE POLICY — Expense Claim Procedure: To claim reimbursement, employees must fill out an Expense claim form or voucher and have it signed by their reporting superior. Approved forms and supporting bills/receipts must be submitted to the accounts department. Claims are reimbursed once a week.
3.9 WORKING HOURS POLICY — Adherence and Discipline: Employees are required to adhere to timings as notified from time to time. HODs and Regional Managers schedule work to meet quality and operational requirements. Habitual late reporting or excessive absenteeism is treated as indiscipline and can lead to disciplinary action, including termination. Attendance and punctuality records are significant factors in performance evaluations, promotions, increments, and transfers. Absence for more than ten consecutive working days without prior leave approval triggers disciplinary action.
3.10 WORKING HOURS POLICY — Standard Hours and Weekly Offs: Working hours are 09.30 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., Monday to Friday. Every Saturday and Sunday is a weekly off. If required for business exigencies, employees cannot refuse Saturday duty. Saturday work entitles an employee to a compensatory off, which must be used within the next five days or it lapses automatically.
3.10 WORKING HOURS POLICY — Grace Period and Penalties: A 10-minute grace period is permitted from 09:30 hrs (until 09:40 hrs). Employees arriving between 09:41 and 09:45 must report to the Director General for approval. Late marks are recorded starting from 09:46 hrs. Three late marks in a month result in the deduction of one day of Casual Leave (CL), or one day's salary if CL is exhausted.
3.10 WORKING HOURS POLICY — Lunch and Regional Office Guidelines: All employees are provided 30 minutes for lunch each day from 1.00 p.m. to 1.30 p.m., and this hour is not extendable. Regional offices also operate from 09:30 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. with the same lunch break and observe Saturday and Sunday as weekly offs. The Council can declare Saturdays as official working days or cancel official holidays during work exigencies.
4. CODE OF CONDUCT — General Principles: AGC is committed to a high Standard of Excellence, expecting the highest standards of ethics, honesty, and professional behavior from all employees. Good personal conduct is essential for a positive work environment, and the Code of Conduct serves as a formal disclosure of Council operations binding on everyone.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Preamble and Philosophy: The Council's philosophy on corporate governance is built on a legacy of fair and transparent governance, disclosure practices, respect for human values, and individual dignity. Employees are guided by the vision statement and values to maintain the success and reputation of AGC. This code is applicable to all staff of the Council.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Honesty, Integrity, and Ethics: All employees must act in conformity with professional standards of personal integrity and honesty, especially on official work, within office premises, or at sponsored events. Ethical conduct must be free from fraud and deception, including the management of actual or potential conflicts of interest.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Respect for Individuals: Employees must treat colleagues and members with dignity and respect, regardless of caste, creed, gender, religion, region, nationality, appearance, or disability. Superiors are encouraged to allow subordinates to express professional views candidly in meetings without fear. Performance feedback should be provided in private and characterized by equity and fairness.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Workplace Communication and Instigation: The use of abusive or offensive language, tones, or gestures is strictly prohibited. Superiors must not insult or demean subordinates. Employees must desist from writing or instigating letters to the Ministry, COA, or any other party; such actions will lead to strict disciplinary action, including termination.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Sharing of Official Information: Employees must not communicate sensitive or confidential information to third parties unless authorized for business reasons. Official communication with the media must be handled only by designated spokespersons. Speaking ill of the Council to external agencies or indulging in rumor-mongering detrimental to Council interests is strictly prohibited.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Maintaining Confidentiality: Employees must maintain the integrity of internal communications and mitigate risks like fact misrepresentation or leakage of price-sensitive information. Information obtained during employment that is not generally known must be kept strictly confidential. This duty of confidentiality applies both during employment and post-separation.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Professional Engagement and Public Advocacy: Employees are encouraged to associate with professional bodies to enrich knowledge and build the Council's image. However, these engagements must not result in a dereliction of duty, discredit the Council's reputation, or abuse Council resources.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Intellectual Property Rights: Any knowledge, inventions, discoveries, or processes gathered by staff during their employment must be shared only with the Council and will become the Council's exclusive property.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Use of Council Assets and Brand Logo: Council resources, including tangible assets (equipment) and intangible assets (designs, software, network), must not be exploited for personal gain. The brand logo must be used without modification, following specific guidelines for visiting cards and other applications; deviations from templates are not permitted.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Information Technology and Email Usage: IT facilities and E-mail must be used judiciously in accordance with Council policy. Users must comply with Corporate E-mail usage Guidelines to ensure resource utilization and prevent undesirable fallouts.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Working Environment and Dress Code: Employees must not engage in activities that disturb peace, harmony, or create tension and violence in the workplace. A formal dress code is required: Male employees wear formal shirts, trousers, and suits/blazers; Female employees wear sarees, salwar kameez, Punjabi suits, blazers, or western formals. Personal grooming and hygiene must be maintained.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Environment, Health & Safety (EHS): Employees must keep office surroundings clean and ensure no inflammable or fire-hazardous materials are stored in premises. Exit passages must remain clutter-free. Fire extinguishers are checked regularly, and staff must immediately raise any safety issues; regular fire drills are administered.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Corporate HR Policy and Learning: AGC is committed to acquiring and retaining high-caliber talent while empowering employees to be innovative. The Council establishes systems for transparency and fairness while creating a culture of continuous learning and excellence.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Risk Management and Prohibited Items: Employees must comply with all applicable laws and the Risk Management framework. The use, sale, purchase, or possession of narcotic drugs and arms/weapons on duty is strictly prohibited. Alcohol consumption during office hours or official meetings is forbidden unless approved by the Director General for extraordinary situations.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Disciplinary Action and Warning Procedure: Violating the code of conduct triggers disciplinary action, including internal memos, warnings, penalties, or dismissal. The warning procedure has stages, though the Council reserves the right to jump stages depending on the case.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Written Warning Stages: (a) Internal Memo: A written warning for misconduct; (b) First Written Warning: For serious breaches or repeated offenses after verbal warnings; (c) Final Written Warning: For significant breaches or failure to improve, outlining required improvements and a warning that further misconduct will result in dismissal. Copies are kept in personnel files.
4.1 CODE OF CONDUCT — Summary Dismissal: In cases of gross misconduct, the penalty is normally summary dismissal without notice. This decision is taken jointly by the Director General in consultation with the Chairman, following prevalent Labor Laws.
4.2 ARMS LENGTH POLICY — Objectives and Hiring Restrictions: This policy prevents conflicts of interest regarding employees with personal businesses or relatives aligned with Council activities. The Council will not hire, empanel, or associate with any vendor or agency owned or partnered by an employee directly or indirectly. Business associations with family members or relatives of employees are also prohibited.
4.3 PREVENTION OF SEXUAL HARASSMENT POLICY: AGC provides an effective complaint Redressal mechanism in accordance with the (Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013). This policy aims to ensure a workplace free of harassment for all women employees.
5. RECRUITMENT POLICY — Objectives: This policy aims to maintain high standards of professional recruitment by encouraging adherence to best practices. The primary goals are to attract and retain a highly qualified, talented workforce based on merit and to disseminate employment opportunities through various channels to ensure a strong talent pool. This policy is applicable to all employees at all levels.
5. RECRUITMENT POLICY — Employment Application Form: Every candidate being considered for an interview, regardless of the source or position, must fill out the Employment Application Form. If any details in the form are found to be false, inaccurate, or if relevant information was deliberately suppressed, the employee's services can be terminated without notice.
5. RECRUITMENT POLICY — Manpower Planning: The recruitment policy must align with the Council’s Manpower Planning. Departmental and Regional Heads systematically review business plans, team structures, and skill requirements to determine manpower needs for the financial year. Based on this assessment, they must fill out a Manpower Requisition Form.
5. RECRUITMENT POLICY — Requisition and Approval: Creating a new position or filling an old one requires approval from the Director General, as it involves financial liability for the Council. Approved Manpower Requisition Forms are submitted to the HR department along with a detailed Job Description.
5. RECRUITMENT POLICY — Sourcing and Screening: The HR Department utilizes various sources to fill positions. Resumes are screened by HR based on the (Job Description). Resumes that meet the job criteria are then forwarded to the respective Head of Department for endorsement.
5. RECRUITMENT POLICY — Interview Scheduling: In consultation with the HOD, HR communicates the interview schedule to shortlisted candidates via phone or email. This communication clearly mentions the contact person, date, time, and venue. If preliminary testing or screening raises doubts about a candidate’s potential, they may be dropped from the process and informed accordingly.
5. RECRUITMENT POLICY — Interview Panel: The interview panel typically includes personnel from two levels above the position being filled (e.g., Directors, HR Manager, and Director General). For senior level recruitment or critical positions, the panel may involve the Executive Director, Director General, Chairman, or outside specialists.
5. RECRUITMENT POLICY — Expenses and Selection Methodology: The Council does not reimburse travel expenses for candidates attending interviews unless specifically approved by the Director General as a special case. Final interviews may be conducted in two or three parts with panelists of increasing seniority. Psychometric tests may be conducted for senior or critical positions depending on the situation.
5. RECRUITMENT POLICY — Fair Selection and Conduct: The HR Head or a nominee must be present at all interviews to ensure communication between panelists and assist with administrative recording. Candidates found canvassing for their own candidature will be disqualified. No recommendations by current employees are entertained to ensure a fair selection process based purely on merit.
5. RECRUITMENT POLICY — HR Department Responsibilities: HR is responsible for communicating policy requirements and procedures to applicants and confidentially storing all associated documentation. Following a candidate’s joining, HR conducts background verifications—including certificates, previous employer checks, address proof, and police/criminal background checks—through a reputed screening company.
5. RECRUITMENT POLICY — Managerial Responsibilities: Heads of Department and Managers are responsible for ensuring that no applicant starts work until their appointment has been formally confirmed.
6. INDUCTION POLICY — Objective and Integration: AGC realizes that to maintain effective and efficient performance, all employees must go through a comprehensive induction process. The Council commits itself to assist new employees in integrating into the culture and acquainting themselves with job requirements as quickly as possible. A formal induction checklist is used to guide HR functionaries through the orientation process. This policy was proposed on 10/02/2022 and has been effective since 08/01/2024.
6. INDUCTION POLICY — Applicability and Significance: This policy applies to all new joiners at the Council. Induction is not viewed as a routine event or mere formality, but as a significant process for the employee to learn about the Council, internalize social and behavioral values, and gain required job-related skills. No new employee shall be engaged on a normal job directly without first being exposed to an Induction Program.
6. INDUCTION POLICY — HR Responsibility and Duration: The Human Resource Department is responsible for planning and delivering a suitable induction program in consultation with the Manager. The standard duration of the induction is two days. This period can be extended if necessary to ensure that the new employee is properly inducted into their role.
6. INDUCTION POLICY — Departmental Introductions and Tour: HR takes the initiative to prepare a brief induction or familiarization program involving introductions to other departments at the location, based on the availability of concerned staff. Such inductions may precede joining a department or occur during the initial working period. A formal announcement is made via email across the Council with a brief introduction of the new employee. The employee is taken around the office to see location utilities and is introduced to all staff.
6. INDUCTION POLICY — Managerial and Verification Requirements: The concerned Reporting Manager is responsible for subsequent induction processes. No employee is allowed to join duties until all requisite documentation has been submitted and background verification is processed through an external professional agency. The employee is required to complete the documentation process during the induction program.
6. INDUCTION POLICY — Required Personnel Documentation: Employees must complete and submit a Personnel Detail Form. Essential documents must be verified against originals, including: SSC and HSC certificates; Degree and Diploma certificates; Work Experience or Relieving letters from previous employers; passport (if available); and PAN/Aadhar Card or Residence Address proof. Three passport-size photographs are also required.
6. INDUCTION POLICY — Final Formalities and Regional Scope: New employees must complete and submit the salary account opening form. All rules defined in the Induction Policy are equally applicable to new employees joining at Regional Offices.
7. PROBATION POLICY — Objective and Evaluation: The objective of the probation period is to allow the Council to closely evaluate and monitor the knowledge, skills, and attitude required for the job. This includes assessing the suitability and efficiency of newly hired employees within the context of the Council. This policy applies to all new joiners.
7. PROBATION POLICY — Duration and Effective Dates: All new entrants are on probation for a period of six months from their date of joining. Employees joining between the 1st and 14th of a month are confirmed effective the 1st, while those joining between the 15th and the last day of the month are confirmed effective the 15th.
7. PROBATION POLICY — Extension and Termination: If performance is not satisfactory, the probation period can be extended for three months. The total period of probation, including extensions, must not exceed nine months. If performance remains unsatisfactory at the end of the extension, services will be terminated. On completion of probation, services are either confirmed, extended, or terminated based on performance.
7. PROBATION POLICY — Performance Feedback: Employees not meeting expected levels must have at least one performance discussion during the probation period with their respective Functional Head. Feedback regarding performance levels and development plans must be discussed with the employee. The same appraisal form used for annual reviews is used for confirmation appraisals.
7. PROBATION POLICY — Confirmation Formalities: Confirmation is not automatic and requires the involvement of HODs and the employee. HR ensures the appraisal and confirmation are completed in a timely manner. A formal letter of confirmation is issued to the confirmed employee by the HR department.
7. PROBATION POLICY — Notice Period and Separation: In the event of separation during the probation period, all probationers are required to serve a notice period as specified in the terms and conditions of their appointment letter.
7. PROBATION POLICY — Leave Restrictions: The probation period serves to help joinees settle and allow the Council to judge their performance and cultural fit. Consequently, no leave is granted during probation unless it is justified as a special case by the Departmental Head. Any leave granted will extend the probation period by the corresponding number of days.
8. LEAVE POLICY — Objective and Eligibility: All employees of AGC are encouraged to avail the paid leave facility for rest and personal exigencies. The policy defines leave eligibility and facilitates systematic planning. Leaves are allotted without discrimination between Management, staff, or other categories. The number of days for leave eligibility per year does not vary with years of service.
8.1 LEAVE POLICY — Annual Allotment Table: All employees except temporary staff are eligible for 30 days of Privilege Leave (PL), 10 days of Casual Leave (CL), and 15 days of Commuted/Sick Leave (SL). Additionally, employees receive 17 National Holidays in liaison with Government Holidays.
8.2 LEAVE POLICY — General Accounting Guidelines: The leave year for accounting purposes is the Calendar year, from January to December. For employees who have completed at least one year of service, 30 days of Privilege Leave are credited to their account on 1st January every year. Casual Leave is also credited at the start of each calendar year. For employees joining mid-year, eligibility is calculated on a pro-rated basis.
8.2 LEAVE POLICY — Sanctioning and Rights: Prefixing or suffixing of leave is permitted, and intervening weekly offs or Public Holidays are counted as leave. The responsibility for obtaining leave sanction lies with the employee. No leave can be claimed as a matter of right. The employee's superior has the discretion to sanction, defer, or refuse leave based on work exigencies.
8.2 LEAVE POLICY — Approval and Payroll Cut-off: Leave must generally be approved with prior intimation to the concerned HOD; otherwise, it is classified as unauthorized and pay may be deducted. Approved leave applications must reach HR by the 25th of every month. Failure to regularize absence before this cut-off results in the absence being treated as unauthorized with no salary paid for that period. Leave records are maintained by the Payroll/HR Department.
8.2 LEAVE POLICY — Leave during Notice Period: No leave can be availed during the notice period. An employee cannot be on leave on their last working day in the Council. If an employee on notice period takes leave due to health or emergency, the notice period is extended by the number of days absent. Leave applications for Regional Heads should be routed to the Director General.
8.3 PRIVILEGE LEAVE (PL) — Eligibility and Probation: All confirmed employees are eligible for paid leave. Employees on probation are not entitled to leave except for exigencies. However, upon confirmation, they can avail of the leave accumulated during the probation period.
8.3 PRIVILEGE LEAVE (PL) — Entitlement and Usage: Every employee is entitled to 30 days of PL per year, credited at the beginning of the calendar year. New joiners can only avail of PL after completing six months of service. An employee cannot avail more than 15 days of PL at one stretch unless approved by the Director General with justification. PL applications must be submitted at least 15 days in advance, though this can be reduced for contingencies.
8.3 PRIVILEGE LEAVE (PL) — Accumulation and Encashment: PL can be carried forward and accumulated up to a maximum of 120 days, which includes the new calendar year entitlement. Permanent employees can encash a maximum of 15 days of PL in a calendar year based on Basic Salary. The calculation formula is (Basic Salary/30 * no. of days). Encashment requests are considered once per calendar year and for a maximum of 30 days. Encashment is taxable and paid with the monthly salary.
8.3 PRIVILEGE LEAVE (PL) — Separation Encashment: In cases of retirement, termination, or resignation, employees are paid the balance of PL to their credit, up to a maximum of 120 days, minus any days already encashed. En-cashed leave is treated as a separate amount and does not form part of the salary or other allowances.
8.4 CASUAL LEAVE (CL) — Eligibility and Credits: All employees are eligible for 10 days of CL per calendar year, credited in advance on 1st January. Mid-year joiners receive pro-rata CL until December 31st. If an employee separates during the year, any excess CL availed will be recovered.
8.4 CASUAL LEAVE (CL) — Usage and Constraints: A maximum of 2 days of CL can be taken at one stretch. CL cannot be combined with PL by any means. If absence exceeds 2 days, the employee must apply for PL. Intervening weekly offs or holidays count as part of the CL, but preceding or succeeding ones do not. Unutilized CL at the end of the year will lapse.
8.4 CASUAL LEAVE (CL) — Half Day and Unscheduled Absence: Half-day CL is permitted provided the employee works a minimum of 4 hours; otherwise, it is calculated as a full day. For unscheduled absence, the reporting manager must be notified on the same day via phone, SMS, or email, subject to supervisor approval. CL cannot be encashed or adjusted against the notice period. Regularization must occur upon resuming duty.
8.5 SICK LEAVE (SL) — Eligibility and Crediting: All confirmed employees are credited with 15 days of SL in advance on 1st January each year. Employees joining mid-year receive pro-rata SL subject to confirmation. If SL is exhausted, excess absence is deducted from PL or treated as unpaid leave.
8.5 SICK LEAVE (SL) — Medical Certification and Entitlement: A medical certificate is required if SL exceeds 2 days. Intervening weekly offs or holidays count as SL. Half-day SL is permitted. SL cannot be encashed or adjusted against the notice period. Sickness must be notified to the reporting officer on the same day by phone. Absence exceeding one week requires a fitness certificate from an attending physician.
8.6 EXTRAORDINARY LEAVE (LWP) and CANCELLATION: The Director General may grant extraordinary leave with or without pay under special circumstances if no other leave is in credit. The Director General can cancel sanctioned leave based on situational needs. If an employee avails of cancelled leave, it is treated as absence from duty and Leave Without Pay (LWP) rules apply.
8.7 COMPENSATORY OFF (Comp-Off) — Entitlement: Employees not entitled to overtime receive a one-day compensatory off for every holiday worked, provided they work at least 6 hours. Saturday work also entitles the employee to a comp-off. Comp-off standing in credit on 31st December must be consumed by the following calendar year; no further carry-forward is allowed.
8.7 COMPENSATORY OFF (Comp-Off) — Procedures and Travel: Holiday work requires written permission from the Director General. Requests for comp-off must be made using the (Leave application form) and presented to the superior for approval and HR record. Employees traveling or returning from official trips on weekly offs or holidays before 12 noon are not eligible for comp-off.
8.7 OVERTIME — Eligibility: Only Office Assistant staff (Driver & Peon) are eligible for overtime benefits. Overtime eligibility begins only after working hours exceed 9 hours as per law.
8.8 MATERNITY and PATERNITY LEAVE: Female employees can avail of Maternity Leave as per the Maternity Benefit Act 1961. Male employees can avail of Paternity Leave as per the Paternity Benefit Act 2017.
8.9 NATIONAL HOLIDAYS — Paid Holidays: The HR department circulates a list of public holidays at the start of the year. Paid holidays should not exceed 11 days annually, standardized across Regional and Head Offices. Four mandatory national holidays include Republic Day (26th Jan), Labour Day (1st May), Independence Day (15th Aug), and Gandhi Jayanti (2nd Oct). Regional Heads decide on 2 holidays in consultation with staff, subject to DG approval.
9.1 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Introduction and Philosophy: The Performance Management System (PMS) is considered the most important element for driving Council excellence, described as the (heart) of the organization. Much like a heart pumps fresh blood, the PMS receives performance data and pumps back milestones in the form of Key Result Areas (KRAs) and training needed to sharpen knowledge, skills, and attitude. It continuously monitors performance to keep the Council healthy and alert.
9.1 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Competence and Dimensions: A professional PMS captures employee competence, which is a combination of job knowledge, skills, and attitude. Performance is evaluated in a three-dimensional structure: individual employee performance, departmental contribution, and the Council's overall business performance in terms of revenue and profits.
9.2 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Primary Objective: The objective of the PMS is to create an integrated process for objective evaluation and merit-based assessment. This institutionalized system supports the growth of individual employees, departments, and the Council while helping to create a performance-driven culture.
9.3 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Job Descriptions: AGC maintains accurate job descriptions for all positions to aid in orienting new members, identifying hiring criteria, and setting standards for performance evaluations. HR prepares these descriptions in discussion with the incumbent and the Reporting Manager whenever new positions are created.
9.3 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Job Description Maintenance: Existing job descriptions are reviewed periodically and revised to reflect changes in duties or responsibilities. Employees are expected to help ensure their descriptions are accurate and relevant. However, job descriptions may not cover every assigned task, and additional responsibilities may be assigned as necessary.
9.4 & 9.5 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Evaluation Cycles: The official Performance Management Cycle is divided into Half-Yearly Business Reviews (HYBR). Interim evaluations are conducted half-yearly for both probationary and confirmed employees to advise them of their progress or identify performance problems. Regional office employees attend these reviews via conference calls scheduled by the Head Office.
9.6 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — PIP and Extraordinary Performance: A Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) is developed by the supervisor and HR for employees needing competency development; the employee must receive written information about the plan. Supervisors are also encouraged to document extraordinary performance incidents. These documents are signed by the HOD, shared with the employee, and used during the annual performance evaluation.
9.7 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Annual Review and Self-Appraisal: The HR Department distributes Self-Appraisal Forms according to a specified schedule. Every appraisee is provided the opportunity to provide a self-assessment of their job performance against set standards. These forms are kept highly confidential and discussed only with HR personnel.
9.7 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Rating Definitions (1–3): Appraisees are rated in five categories: Rating 1: Unacceptable Performance (even with coaching, performance does not meet expectations; requires immediate improvement or termination). Rating 2: Needs Improvements (performance does not consistently meet expectations; PIP required). Rating 3: Satisfactory Performance (consistently fulfills and at times exceeds expectations).
9.7 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Rating Definitions (4–5): Rating 4: Good Performance (exceeds expectations in several areas on a regular basis). Rating 5: Excellent Performance (consistently and significantly exceeds expectations in all areas).
9.7 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Record Keeping and IDP: Completed Annual Performance Evaluation Forms are official personnel documents maintained by HR in official files. All review documents are treated as (Strictly Confidential). HR uses this data for training needs analysis and creates Individual Development Plans (IDP) for critical roles or those with ratings of 1 and 2.
9.7 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Final Approvals: A synopsis of the appraisal process and salary increment recommendations is sent to the Chairman and Vice Chairman for approval. Once approved, salary increments are processed, including arrears if applicable. The Chairman and Vice Chairman also approve the Annual Training calendar and Employee Engagement Plan arising from the PMS.
9.7 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Phase 1: Preparation and Research: This phase involves meetings with top management and employees to understand Council legacy, strengths, and goals. HR studies the previous year's business plan, compensation philosophy, and Council structure. It also includes studying past PMS outcomes and conducting compensation surveys of the sector.
9.7 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Phase 1: Engagement and Goal Setting: Phase 1 includes conducting (Goal setting) offsite workshops for senior and mid-management to define KRAs and feedback techniques. It also covers monthly review meetings, counseling for underperforming employees, and monthly Town Hall meetings chaired by the DG or Chairman to communicate business performance and challenges.
9.7 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Phase 2: Design and Distribution: Based on stakeholder input, a customized PMS and appraisal form are designed, including sections for KRAs, enabling factors, training needs, and supervisor comments. HR explains the design to HODs and distributes forms to all eligible employees online. Supervisors conduct one-on-one meetings with staff to set KRAs for the next year.
9.7 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Phase 2: Analysis and Strategy: HR performs a quality check on completed documents and analyzes the (Bell Curve) for departments and the Council, making corrections as required. Strategies for salary increments and performance bonus formulas are developed. The final outcome is presented to the COA for review and approval of increments, bonuses, and promotions.
9.7 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT — Phase 3: Final Execution: HR prepares department-wise salary increments and the annual training calendar. A revised CTC structure is created, and payroll is processed based on revised compensation. Salary increment/promotion letters are distributed to employees with copies to HODs, and individuals are informed of their revised KRAs.
10.1 L&D — Objective: AGC encourages employee development and intends to provide periodical training for the development of employees. This training is designed to enhance specific job skills, overall professional strengths, and personal development.
10.2 L&D — Core Principles (Responsibility and Performance): All training, learning, and development must lead to improvements in employees and their performance. Learning is considered everyone's responsibility, and all employees are required to support and promote growth in line with business requirements.
10.2 L&D — Core Principles (Leadership and Culture): The support and active participation of senior managers is essential in both planning and managing L&D as leaders of a culture of continuous improvement. The role of HR is to facilitate change, manage L&D activity, and build the overall learning culture rather than just coordinating training. Learning is a core part of the Council's value system.
10.3 L&D — Training Budget: The Training Budget is a centralized budget. It is initiated by the HR department and requires approval from the Executive Director (ED).
10.3 L&D — Area of Focus (Functional): One primary focus is to equip employees to perform daily tasks and jobs effectively through adequate functional training. This ensures employees are exposed to and updated with the latest trends and technology in their specific functional domain.
10.3 L&D — Area of Focus (Personal and Organizational): Learning plans also focus on training related to behaviors and attitudes to help employees be personally effective and grow as individuals. Furthermore, learning initiatives are derived from the Council's direction and strategy to help transform the organization and achieve its vision.
10.3 L&D — Identification of Learning Needs: The process starts with identifying training needs at two levels: (i) Behavioral Training Needs and (ii) Functional/Technical Training Needs.
10.3 L&D — Behavioral Training Needs Identification: Behavioral needs for individual employees are generally identified through the Performance Appraisal Process. They can also be identified via inputs from Functional Heads. Priority is given to the top 2-3 behavioral needs identified across different levels while keeping budgets and relevance in mind. These programs aim to increase productivity and personality growth.
10.3 L&D — Functional and Technical Needs Identification: These needs are identified in line with operations undertaken by various departments. Identification occurs after discussions with the respective HOD or through the Performance Appraisal Process. The identification survey is conducted once a year, but can be repeated if business needs change.
10.3 L&D — Functional Training Programs: These programs are designed to provide all employees with both an in-depth and overview look at departmental operations and necessary skill sets. Additionally, team members may be nominated for seminars and lectures as required for skill updating.
10.3 L&D — Training Nomination Procedure: Employees may self-nominate with approval from their respective HODs. Alternatively, HODs or Directors can nominate specific employees or teams. The Head of the respective department and the ED have final authority on nominating individuals or groups. HR verifies and processes these nominations.
10.3 L&D — Eligibility Conditions for Training: A team member is eligible to attend a training program only if: (i) They have not attended a similar program in the previous 6 months; (ii) They are not the only person who has been trained in a sequence of external programs continuously in the last 6 months; (iii) They are not currently facing disciplinary action related to performance or behavior; and (iv) They are not serving a notice period.
10.4 L&D — Infrastructure and Internal Faculty: Adequate infrastructure is provided for training programs as needed. Internal Faculty consists of experienced team members or leaders with good communication skills, identified by HR to be developed as subject matter experts.
10.4 L&D — External Faculty and Selection Authority: External Faculty includes training institutes and trainers identified based on prior experience or references from other companies. Specialized resources are used for specialized skills training. The final authority for selecting both faculty and institutes is vested with the ED. Selection tenets include cost-effectiveness, training effectiveness, and continuous availability.
10.4 L&D — Training Feedback: Evaluation of the program is done via feedback from trainees soon after completion. Feedback aspects include: (a) Content of the training program; (b) Coverage of the topic; and (c) Presentation of the subject matter. HR collates this data.
11.1 & 11.2 COMPENSATION — Objective and Individual Packages: AGC believes in paying fair salaries to all employees to encourage business results and retain talent. Recruitment and compensation are based on skill sets, experience, competencies, and market potential. Compensation packages are individual and specific based on agreed terms. All increments and compensation are merit-based.
11.2 COMPENSATION — Confidentiality Rule: Confidentiality of compensation must be nurtured, demonstrated, and communicated to all employees. Comparing salaries between employees is strictly prohibited.
11.3 COMPENSATION — Grade Structure and Designations: The Council utilizes a specific grade table: M1-M2 (Director General, Executive Director); M3-M5 (Sr. Director, Director, Jt. Director); M6-M8 (Regional, Deputy, or Assistant Director); M9-M10 (Sr./Jr. Executive Officer); M11-M13 (Sr./Jr. Executive, Management Trainee); E1 (Front Office Receptionist); E2 (Office Assistant).
11.4 COMPENSATION — Payment Terms: Salaries are credited on the 30th of every month, except in unavoidable circumstances which will be communicated to employees. Salary slips are generated for each payment. Benefits are provided as applicable to the position and as mentioned in the appointment or salary revision letter.
11.5 COMPENSATION — Salary Components and Basic Pay: The compensation package is reckoned on a Cost to Council (CTC) basis. Basic Salary is the money received for rendering services and is the amount received for work done over a day, week, or month. The basic scale is determined by terms of employment and revisions and is used to calculate PF, ESIC, and Gratuity.
11.5 COMPENSATION — HRA, Transport, and Special Allowances: House Rent Allowance (HRA) is paid monthly as per grade but is proportionately reduced for any absence without leave. Transport Allowance is provided for commuting between residence and office. Special Allowance is also a core component of the broad compensation package.
11.5 COMPENSATION — Leave Travel Concession (LTC) Eligibility: Confirmed employees who have completed at least one year of uninterrupted service are eligible for LTC. It is paid as one month of Basic salary in a block of two years. Both the employee and their family are entitled to LTC.
11.5 COMPENSATION — LTC Travel Rules: Employees may use any mode of transport, but reimbursement is restricted based on Basic salary entitlements. Travel outside of India is not considered for tax exemption.
11.5 COMPENSATION — Gratuity and Provident Fund (PF): All permanent employees are members of the Group Gratuity Scheme, governed by the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972. Employees join the PF as stipulated in the 1952 Act. This is a contributory fund for monetary benefits post-retirement. The fund is administered by the Regional Provident Fund Commissioners Office.
11.5 COMPENSATION — PF Procedures: New employees must fill a nomination form for the HR department. Joinees from other organizations must fill forms provided by HR to transfer their PF account from previous employers to AGC. RPF may permit withdrawals based on prevailing rules. HR reviews and approves any individual contract exceptions.
11.5 COMPENSATION — Group Mediclaim Policy: The Council provides Mediclaim insurance coverage for hospitalization needs, with premiums borne by the Council. It applies to all regular employees and covers the employee, spouse, and two dependent children up to age 25. No medical check-up is required upon joining the scheme.
11.5 COMPENSATION — Mediclaim Grade-wise Limits: Reimbursable limits are as follows: Grade M1-M2 (Rs. 10 lacs); Grade M3-M5 (Rs. 8 lacs); Grade M6-M8 (Rs. 6 lacs); All other Grades (Rs. 4 lacs). Benefits are subject to the insurance company's terms and conditions.
11.5 COMPENSATION — Children Education Allowance (Standard): Reimbursement for tuition fees and allowance is merged, providing up to Rs. 27,000 per child per annum. This is limited to a maximum of 2 children until age 18 and/or up to the 12th standard. For regular children, the age limit is 20 years or until passing 12th class. There is no minimum age.
11.5 COMPENSATION — Children Education Allowance (Special Children): Reimbursement for special children is a fixed Rs. 4,500 per month. This requires submission of necessary documents. The upper age limit for special children is 22 years.
11.5 COMPENSATION — Liveries: Office Assistants (E2 grade) are entitled to three sets of uniforms (cloth and stitching charges) yearly.
12. LAPTOP/DATACARD & MOBILE PHONE POLICY — Objectives: The objectives of this policy are to: (i) improve accessibility and effectiveness for better employee availability and connectivity; (ii) provide uniform guidelines for entitlement, reimbursement, and cost-effectiveness ; and (iii) improve overall efficiency, productivity, accuracy, and dependency.
12.1 LAPTOP/DATACARD POLICY — Eligibility and Approval: Laptops are provided by the Council to selected staff based on their job profile for discharging official duties, requiring Director General approval. Data Cards are provided only to employees in business-critical roles to facilitate communication while on the move, also requiring prior approval from the Director.
12.1 LAPTOP/DATACARD POLICY — Purchase and Replacement Procedure: To issue a laptop, computer, or data card, a Purchase Requisition must be raised by the Functional Head and submitted to the Executive Director (ED). Replacement or upgrades are done based on an IT Department technical assessment, not before 3 years of use, and require prior Director General approval. Employees must surrender the old laptop before receiving a new one.
12.1 LAPTOP/DATACARD POLICY — Theft, Loss, and Liability: In case of theft, the user must lodge a police complaint (FIR) and forward it to the IT department through their Functional Head. Employees are financially liable for any loss or damage to the laptop.
12.1 LAPTOP/DATACARD POLICY — Separation and Return: Upon retirement or resignation, employees must return the laptop and all accessories in good condition to the IT department. If the device is found damaged beyond normal wear and tear, the estimated repair cost is recoverable from the employee's full and final settlement.
12.1 LAPTOP/DATACARD POLICY — Usage Caution: Employees must use laptops only for authorized purposes. Watching unauthorized sites such as sports, online games, or pornographic sites is strictly prohibited. Strict disciplinary action is taken against any employee found guilty of such usage.
12.2 MOBILE PHONE POLICY — Eligibility and Approval: Employees who are largely mobile for out-of-office duties, need to remain accessible, or operate from multiple locations are eligible for mobile facilities. Final approval from the Director General is required.
12.2 MOBILE PHONE POLICY — Monthly Reimbursement Limits: Entitlements include rentals and all facilities (STD/ISD/Roaming, internet, ringtones). Limits are: Grades M1 to M5 (Rs. 2000/-); Grades M6 to M10 (Rs. 1000/-); Grades M11 to M13 (Rs. 800/-). Staff in grades E1-E2 are reimbursed up to Rs. 300/- for calls.
12.2 MOBILE PHONE POLICY — Ground Rules and Verification: Mobile handsets are standard equipment issued by the administration department based on HOD requisitions. Employees can transfer existing numbers to the Council plan at their own expense. AGC reserves the right to dial any doubtful number from a user's bill for verification without legal liability for privacy violation. Activity is monitored, and employees must return phones and SIM cards upon resignation or retirement.
12.2 MOBILE PHONE POLICY — Loss Penalties: (i) First instance: Recovery of depreciated cost or a minimum Rs. 500/- penalty if the depreciated value is zero; (ii) Second instance: Recovery of the purchase value of a similar functionality handset; (iii) Third instance: Recovery of purchase value plus a Rs. 1000/- penalty.
12.2 MOBILE PHONE POLICY — Authorized Usage and Restrictions: (i) Stolen/lost phones must be reported to Admin for deactivation and an FIR lodged; (ii) Phones must not be used for non-Council commercial purposes or unlawful acts; (iii) Hand-held phones must not be used while driving; (iv) Recording or photography is restricted to Council events; (v) Downloading unauthorized software, games, or sports/pornographic material is strictly prohibited and attracts disciplinary action.
12.2 MOBILE PHONE POLICY — Personal Use and Upgrades: Personal use is permitted within reasonable limits. If an employee exceeds monthly limits for personal calls, they must obtain a waiver from their HOD for Finance, otherwise, the excess is deducted from their salary. Employees are eligible for a new mobile handset after 4 years.
13.1 TRAVEL POLICY — General Objective: The policy aims to provide employees with comfortable travel while on Council business. All travel must be undertaken only after approval from the department or functional head. A minimum of 3 working days is required for the approval process. Any deviations require Director General approval.
13.1 TRAVEL POLICY — Overseas Travel: All international travel is governed by the MDA scheme revised guidelines effective from 1st April 2006. Overseas travel follows the Government of India policy in force at the time of travel. Deviations are subject to Director General approval.
13.1 TRAVEL POLICY — Domestic City Classification: Cities are classified for reimbursement purposes: Class A (Mumbai, Delhi & NCR, Kolkata, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad, Ahmedabad); Class B (All other State and UT Capitals, Pune, Surat); Class C (All other cities and towns).
13.2 TRAVEL RULES — Prior Approval and Bookings: All travel requires written prior approval from the Director General, specifically for air travel. Bookings and cancellations must be routed through the Travel/Administration Department. Requisitions must be submitted 3 working days in advance for domestic travel and 10 working days for international travel.
13.2 TRAVEL RULES — Ticket Reimbursement and Cancellation: Tickets are normally purchased through the Council's appointed travel agent. If an employee books a ticket personally, it must be cheaper than the agent's rate, and the used ticket stub or boarding pass must be attached for reimbursement. Last-minute cancellations are the employee's responsibility, and they must bear the cost if the cancellation is due to personal reasons. Unused tickets must be surrendered immediately upon return for refund.
13.2 TRAVEL RULES — Travel Advances (Procedure and Reporting): Advances can be requested 3 days (domestic) or 10 days (international) before travel via a requisition form. Expense statements must be submitted within one week of return or before the next visit. Until then, ticket costs are held as an (advance) against the employee.
13.2 TRAVEL RULES — Travel Advance Limits Table: (i) Travel for a day: Grades M1-M6 (Rs. 5000); Others (Rs. 3000). (ii) Up to 3 days: Grades M1-M3 (Rs. 10,000); M4-M6 (Rs. 7000); Others (Rs. 6000). (iii) 4 days to a week: Grades M1-M3 (Rs. 15,000); M4-M6 (Rs. 10,000); Others (Rs. 8000). Advances above these limits require DG approval with reasons.
13.2 TRAVEL RULES — Reimbursable Expenses (Medical and Entertainment): Actual medical expenses for sudden illness during domestic travel are reimbursed on production of bills. Entertainment expenses for members or Ministry officials over meals or taxi for official purposes are reimbursed upon DG approval.
13.2 TRAVEL RULES — Credit Card Usage: Employees using credit cards for travel bills must enclose charge slips with the Travel Statement Form for Council payment. Interest or service charges resulting from payment delays are not reimbursed and must be borne by the employee.
13.2 TRAVEL RULES — Lodging and Boarding: The Administration department negotiates corporate rates with hotels. Employees are expected to stay in these negotiated hotels if available. If no such hotel exists, employees may choose a hotel based on their entitlements, informing the travel department and demonstrating cost consciousness.
14. LOCAL CONVEYANCE POLICY — Objectives and Discretion: The policy aims to lay down guidelines for all employees to claim expenses incurred for local travel undertaken for official outdoor duties. It expects employees to apply reasonable discretion while keeping policy guidelines in mind at all times. This policy is applicable to all employees.
14.3 LOCAL CONVEYANCE — Personal Vehicle Reimbursement Rates: When using a personal vehicle for Council work with prior approval, reimbursement rates are: (1) Four-wheelers for official visits (Grades M1 to M3) at Rs. 8 per km; (2) Two-wheelers for official visits within the city (Other Grades) at Rs. 4 per km. This rate is inclusive of fuel expenses and maintenance charges.
14.3 LOCAL CONVEYANCE — Vehicle Usage Rules and Parking: Employees may use personal vehicles only upon prior approval from the HOD or Director General. Parking charges are reimbursed only against the presentation of parking tickets. All car hires must be booked through the travel/administration department and must be pre-approved by the Director General or HOD.
14.3 LOCAL CONVEYANCE — Economical Transport and Distance Limits: When not using a personal vehicle, employees must use the most economical means of public transport. If traveling by auto-rickshaw, taxi, or public transport, the maximum limit is 60 km one way. No claims are permitted for travel between home and the office, including for those receiving fuel benefits for Council-owned vehicles.
14.3 LOCAL CONVEYANCE — Outstation and Special Expenses: Functional Heads may authorize other genuine or bonafide expenses if a suitable written explanation is provided. Employees of all grades traveling outstation during weekdays for business must report to the office for at least a half day or a minimum of 4 hours. If travel starts from 5 pm onwards, boarding passes or tickets must be submitted to HR/Admin upon return.
14.3 LOCAL CONVEYANCE — Claim Authorization and Timeline: All claims must be authorized by the ED. It is the joint responsibility of the employee, supervisor, and HOD to verify the correctness of the claim. Reimbursement claims must be made through Cash Vouchers. Once approved by the ED, accounts will clear the claims within 3 working days.
14.3 LOCAL CONVEYANCE — Authorized Mode by Grade: (1) Grades M1 to M4: Taxi (Reimbursed on actuals with receipts); (2) Grades M5 to M9: Auto Rickshaw, Local Train (1st class), or Taxi in areas without autos (Reimbursed on actuals with receipts, or with HOD approval if without receipt); (3) Grades E1 to E3: Bus or Local Train (2nd class) (Reimbursed on actuals with receipts).
15. EMPLOYEE WELFARE — Objective and Engagement: The objective is to engage employees at work to break the monotony of regular tasks. These activities aim to improve overall harmony, productivity, and positivity within the Council. Celebrations include birthdays, an annual picnic, and periodic get-togethers.
15. EMPLOYEE WELFARE — Birthday and Special Events: Birthday celebrations for all employees whose birthdays fall within the month are held on every 4th Friday of that month. (AGC Day) is a designated get-together for all employees. Additionally, employees are taken for an annual picnic once every year.
15. EMPLOYEE WELFARE — Town Hall Meetings (Open Sessions): Periodically, Town Hall Meetings are held at each location to allow employees to seek clarification, provide suggestions, or discuss important business and environment matters. These sessions also allow management to communicate business plans and policies. The Director General chairs sessions at the Head Office, while Regional Directors/HODs chair them at Regional Offices. All sessions are minuted and sent to the Director General.
15. EMPLOYEE WELFARE — Suggestion Scheme: A (Suggestion Box) is placed in a conspicuous location in each AGC office for employees to drop their ideas. These suggestions are reviewed periodically by the HR Department.
15. EMPLOYEE WELFARE — Career Progression Philosophy: The Council ensures the growth of employees alongside the growth of the Council. This is managed through the Performance Management System, providing a detailed career path for every position. Progression is based on merit, including performance, conduct, aptitude, and competence (job knowledge, skills, and attitude).
15. EMPLOYEE WELFARE — Vertical, Horizontal, and Lateral Growth: Career growth follows three paths: (1) Vertical: Promotion to the next level in hierarchy or up-gradation to a higher grade (Career Enrichment); (2) Horizontal: Management of an additional department alongside the existing one (Career Enlargement); (3) Lateral: Promotion followed by responsibility for a different department to strengthen it or bridge critical gaps (Career Enhancement).
15. EMPLOYEE WELFARE — (Commandos) and Strategic Roles: Employees selected for Lateral Career growth are known as (Commandos). They are considered to be on a fast-track career path and are preferred for strategic positions such as HOD or Director General.
15. EMPLOYEE WELFARE — Promotion Prerequisites: Promotion is a privilege, not a time-bound right. Prerequisites include: (a) Graduation for all roles (Science preferred); (b) Post-graduation for senior roles (Manager to DG); (c) A consistent rating of at least 4 (outstanding) in the previous three appraisal cycles. Employees are encouraged to update skills via Council-sponsored training.
15. EMPLOYEE WELFARE — Promotion Selection and Approval: The Council prefers internal talent but will acquire talent externally if internal competence is not appropriate. If multiple aspirants exist for one position, an (Assessment Centre) is used to decide promotions. Promotions are processed by HR, recommended by the Director General, and finally approved by the Chairman and Vice Chairman.
16. GRIEVANCE HANDLING — Objective and Scope: The Council ensures open communication between management and employees. Every employee has the right to lodge a grievance with their Manager or HR if they believe a decision, behavior, or action affecting their employment is unfair. This policy applies to all categories of employees and aims for prompt and effective resolution.
16.3 GRIEVANCE HANDLING — Redressal Hierarchy: The Council follows a four-level hearing authority structure: (Level 1) HOD; (Level 2) HR; (Level 3) Director General with the help of a fact-finding committee; and (Level 4) Chairman.
16.4 GRIEVANCE HANDLING — Policy Exceptions: This grievance procedure is specifically not for the resolution of collective grievances related to salaries or benefits. The Director General is authorized to approve any exceptions to this policy, excluding cases being heard at the Chairman level.
16.5 & 16.6 DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURE — Objectives and Misconduct: The disciplinary procedure provides a framework for instances where employees fail to meet conduct standards. It applies to all employees. Gross misconduct acts that attract disciplinary action include: (a) Insubordination or disobedience; (b) Bribery or illegal gratification; (c) Absence without permission or habitual late attendance; (d) Rude or uncivil behavior; and (e) Neglect of work or consistently poor performance .
16.6 DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURE — Serious Conduct Violations: Further acts of gross misconduct include: (g) Drunkenness on duty; (h) Criminal conviction involving moral turpitude; (i) Unauthorized divulgence of confidential information; (j) Commission of acts subversive to discipline; (k) Failure to give correct history/information when demanded; and (l) Non-compliance with any rule of this HR policy .
16.6 DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURE — Integrity and Safety Violations: Prohibited acts also include: (n) Gambling within Council premises; (o) Theft, fraud, or dishonesty; (q) Sexual or any other type of harassment; (r) Writing defaming pseudonymous letters or emails against stakeholders; (s) Spreading rumors or instigating staff; and (t) Participating in unauthorized meetings during working hours .
16.7 DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURE — Informal Stage: Managers must bring required standards to the employee's attention. Minor misconduct, such as recurrent lateness, should be dealt with informally by the Reporting Manager or HOD without delay. Outcomes should be confirmed in writing. Formal action is invoked if the issue persists, improvements are not achieved, or new information makes the matter sufficiently serious .
16.7 DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURE — Formal Stage and Inquiry: For grave misconduct, the DG or an authorized authority delivers a Charge-Sheet within seven days of the incident. An inquiry is conducted by an appointed Enquiry Committee or HR expert. The inquiry officer submits findings highlighting proved or unproved charges. HR functions as the nodal agency to facilitate this procedure.
16.7 DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURE — Final Decision and Appeals: The decision ratified by the Committee of Administration (COA) or Director General is final and binding. It is communicated formally before action is taken. There is no appeal against the order ratified by the COA except through legal remedies. Failure to follow this process may result in disciplinary action against the personnel involved.
16.7 DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURE — Penalties and Suspension: Proved misconduct penalties include: (i) Suspension (as per law); (ii) Levy of fines; (iii) Stoppage or postponement of increment; (iv) Demotion to a lower post or grade; (v) Reprimand; and (vi) Termination or dismissal . Employees may be suspended pending inquiry with a subsistence allowance. No two forms of punishment shall be given for a single misconduct.
17. EXIT INTERVIEW — Objective and Purpose: The policy provides an opportunity for the DG to conclude an employee's tenure on a positive note. It is used to obtain valuable feedback regarding training, development, benefits, and compensation, allowing the Council to take corrective actions on identified issues. This policy applies to all employees leaving the Council.
17.3 EXIT INTERVIEW — Notification and Scheduling: Once an employee resigns, the Reporting Manager must immediately inform the HR Department. HR then contacts the manager to arrange the exit interview. Employees are required to complete an exit interview and fill in the Exit Interview Form at least 48 hours before their last working day.
17.3 EXIT INTERVIEW — Process and Confidentiality: The interview is an objective process aimed at understanding the root cause of the resignation. It is held confidentially with a member of the HR Department. Feedback is shared with the Functional Head and DG to be considered for process improvement opportunities.
17.3 EXIT INTERVIEW — Resignation Acceptance and Relieving: The reporting superior must communicate the acceptance of resignation in writing, clearly indicating the specific relieving date. A copy of this acceptance letter is forwarded to the HR Manager and HOD.
17.3 EXIT INTERVIEW — Handover of Property: Employees must handover all Council property before their last working day, including data cards, laptops, mobile phones, corporate credit cards, and training materials. Upon receipt of these items, the Manager completes a (No dues Certificate) and forwards it to HR on or before the last working day.
17.3 EXIT INTERVIEW — Systems Deactivation: The Manager is responsible for arranging the deactivation of email and other system access upon receipt of resignation or within one week of the employee's last working day.
18. SEPARATION POLICY — Objective and Grounds: The policy objective is to facilitate a smooth transition for employees while protecting the interests of both the Council and the separating individual. Separation can occur on voluntary grounds (resignation or termination) or involuntary grounds initiated by the Council .
18.2 SEPARATION POLICY — Grounds for Involuntary Separation (Behavior and Performance): Involuntary separation may be initiated for reasons including: (i) non-performance; (ii) financial impropriety; (iii) immodest or unacceptable personal behavior with colleagues; and (iv) harassment or intimidation of subordinates .
18.2 SEPARATION POLICY — Grounds for Involuntary Separation (Conflict and Misconduct): Additional grounds for involuntary separation include: (v) colluding with vendors, suppliers, or agents for personal benefit; (vi) working against the interest of the Council; (vii) working against the interest of the nation; and (viii) general misconduct .
18.3 SEPARATION POLICY — Voluntary Separation Procedure: An employee wishing to quit voluntarily must submit a resignation letter to the HOD and Director General. Concerned parties must discuss and understand the reason for resignation before accepting it on behalf of the Council. The HOD must communicate acceptance in writing, clearly indicating the reason, notice period to be served, and the specific relieving date. Copies of the acceptance letter are forwarded to HR and the Director General.
18.3 SEPARATION POLICY — Involuntary Termination Procedure: If an employee's services are terminated, they are served a notice signed by the Director General under the guidance of the Chairman. This notice includes termination details, reasons, the notice period to be served, and the relieving date. The employee can approach HR for clarification or information regarding the termination.
18.3 SEPARATION POLICY — Retirement from Services: Employees retire from the Council upon attaining the age of 60. All applicable dues are paid upon retirement, and any outstanding dues are recovered from the full and final settlement.
18.3 SEPARATION POLICY — Voluntary Abandonment of Services: An employee is considered to have voluntarily abandoned or relinquished their lien on employment if they are absent for 21 calendar days in a row or more without notification/authorization. This is deemed a repudiation of contract by the employee rather than a termination by the Council. HR follows due process to allow the employee to represent their case before taking them off the payroll. The Council is not liable to pay notice pay in these cases.
19. PANDEMIC & WFH POLICY — Purpose and Scope: The policy outlines active measures to mitigate the spread of coronavirus and ensure a healthy workplace. Employees must follow these rules diligently and respond transparently to health precautions. The Council treats private health data with high confidentiality and sensitivity. The policy is subject to updates based on government guidelines and applies to all employees, including contractual and regional staff .
19.3 PANDEMIC POLICY — Sick Leave and COVID-19 Diagnosis: Employees with cold symptoms (cough, sneezing, fever) or who feel poorly must request sick leave or work from home. If diagnosed with COVID-19, an employee can only return to the office after a 14-day compulsory quarantine and providing a doctor's certificate confirming full recovery.
19.3 PANDEMIC POLICY — WFH for Exposure and Symptoms: Employees with COVID-19 symptoms must isolate and may request to work from home if able. Those in close contact with an infected person must get tested, request WFH, and avoid physical contact with colleagues.
19.3 PANDEMIC POLICY — WFH for Caregivers and Parents: Parents who must stay home with children can request WFH with HOD approval to set performance expectations. Employees caring for an infected family member must work from home and can only return to the office 14 calendar days after the family member's recovery, provided they are asymptomatic or have a doctor's certificate. Physical contact with colleagues is prohibited during this time.
19.3 PANDEMIC POLICY — Work Logs and Commuting: All employees on WFH must maintain a (work log book) and submit it to their supervisor every evening. Employees who normally use public transportation and have no alternatives may request WFH as a precaution. Voluntary travel to high-risk areas requires a 14-day WFH period upon return without physical contact with colleagues.
19.3 PANDEMIC POLICY — Events and Virtual Meetings: The Council may cancel or postpone all work trips and events (domestic and international) at its discretion. In-person meetings, including partner meetings and candidate interviews, should be conducted virtually where possible.
19.3 PANDEMIC POLICY — General Hygiene Rules: Employees must follow the 20-second hand wash method after using the toilet, eating, or coughing/sneezing. Sanitizers are provided around the office. Coughing or sneezing should be done into the sleeve or elbow; tissues must be discarded properly followed by immediate hand sanitization. Windows should be opened regularly for ventilation. Touching the face (eyes, nose, mouth) should be avoided.
19.3 PANDEMIC POLICY — Allowances and Financials: During WFH periods, conveyance and traveling allowances are not paid. Employees granted WFH are eligible for an internet allowance of up to Rs. 500/- per month against submitted bills, subject to HOD approval. The Council may consider other allowances during lockdowns or WFH.
